About the Planets

Our solar system has eight planets and five dwarf planets. It is located in one of the spiral arms of the Milky Way galaxy called the Orion Arm.

PLANETS FACTS

Mercury

Mercury is the smallest planet in our solar system and closest to the Sun. It has a very thin atmosphere and its surface experiences extreme temperature variations.

Learn More

Venus

Venus is the second planet from the Sun and has a thick, toxic atmosphere. It is known for its extreme surface temperatures and volcanic activity.

Learn More

Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known planet to support life. It has a diverse climate and a protective atmosphere.

Learn More

Mars

Mars, also known as the Red Planet, is the fourth planet from the Sun. It has a thin atmosphere and is home to the largest volcano and canyon in the solar system..

Learn More

Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system and fifth from the Sun. It is a gas giant known for its Great Red Spot and many moons.

Learn More

Saturn

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and known for its prominent ring system. It is also a gas giant with numerous moons..

Learn More

Uranus

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun and has a unique sideways rotation. It is an ice giant with a cold atmosphere and faint rings.

Learn More

Neptune

Neptune is the eighth planet from the Sun and is known for its strong winds and storms. It is a gas giant with a deep blue color due to methane in its atmosphere.

Learn More

DWARF PLANETS FACTS

Pluto

Pluto, once considered the ninth planet, is the most well-known dwarf planet. It has a complex surface with mountains, valleys, and plains, and it has five known moons.

Learn More

Eris

Eris is the second-largest dwarf planet and was discovered in 2005. It is slightly smaller than Pluto but has a larger mass. Eris has one known moon named Dysnomia

Learn More

Haumea

Haumea is known for its elongated shape due to its rapid rotation. It is located in the Kuiper Belt and has two moons, Hi'iaka and Namaka.

Learn More

Makemake

Makemake, also located in the Kuiper Belt, is one of the largest known dwarf planets. It has a very low temperature, and its surface is covered with methane, ethane, and possibly nitrogen ices.

Learn More

Ceres

Ceres is the only dwarf planet located in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter. It is the largest object in the asteroid belt and contains water ice beneath its surface.
