U r a n u s

Hey there! I'm Uranus, the icy giant with a quirky spin.

Overview

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun. It has the third-largest planetary radius and fourth-largest planetary mass in the Solar System. Uranus is unique in that it rotates on its side, which means its poles are in the same plane as the rest of the Solar System. It has a faint ring system and a complex atmosphere that includes water, ammonia, and methane.

Physical Characteristics

  • Diameter: 50,724 km
  • Mass: 8.68 × 10^25 kg
  • Average Temperature: -195 °C

Orbit & Rotation

  • Orbital Period: 84 Earth years
  • Rotation Period: 17 hours
  • Distance from Sun: 2.9 billion km

Moons

Uranus has 27 known moons, with the largest being Titania, Oberon, Umbriel, Ariel, and Miranda.

Atmosphere

Ice Giant:Uranus' atmosphere is composed mainly of hydrogen (83%) and helium (15%), with a higher proportion of "ices" such as water, ammonia, and methane.

Temperature Extremes: Upper atmospheric temperatures are around -224°C (-371°F), making it one of the coldest planets.

Exploration history

Voyager 2 (1986): The only spacecraft to fly by Uranus, providing data on its atmosphere, rings, and moons.

Interesting Facts

Axial Tilt: Uranus has an extreme axial tilt of 98 degrees, causing it to essentially roll on its side as it orbits the Sun.

Methane: The presence of methane in its atmosphere gives Uranus its characteristic blue-green color.

Magnetic Field: Uranus has an unusual magnetic field that is tilted 59 degrees from its rotational axis and offset from its center.

Summary

Uranus, with its extreme tilt and unique atmospheric composition, challenges our understanding of planetary formation and dynamics. Its distinct characteristics, observed by Voyager 2, continue to intrigue scientists and drive the need for further exploration.
