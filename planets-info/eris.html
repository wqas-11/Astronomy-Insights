E r i s

Greetings! I'm Eris, the distant and intriguing dwarf planet.

Overview

Eris is the most massive dwarf planet in the solar system and the second-largest by volume. It is a member of the scattered disk, a group of trans-Neptunian objects. Eris's discovery in 2005 led to the reclassification of Pluto as a dwarf planet.

Physical Characteristics

  • Diameter: 2,326 km
  • Mass: 1.66 × 10^22 kg
  • Average Temperature: -243 °C

Orbit & Rotation

  • Orbital Period: 558 Earth years
  • Rotation Period: 25.9 hours
  • Distance from Sun: 10.1 billion km

Moons

Eris has one known moon, Dysnomia.

Atmosphere

Possible Atmosphere: Eris may have a very thin atmosphere that collapses and freezes onto the surface as it moves further from the Sun.

Cold Surface: Eris is one of the coldest objects in the solar system, with temperatures around -243°C (-405°F).

Interesting Facts

Highly Reflective Surface: Eris has a bright, reflective surface, indicating the presence of methane ice.

Moon Dysnomia: Eris has one known moon, Dysnomia, which helps scientists estimate its mass.

Summary

Eris, one of the most distant and coldest known dwarf planets, challenges our understanding of the outer solar system. Its reflective surface and moon Dysnomia provide valuable data for studying these distant objects.
