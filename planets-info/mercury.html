M e r c u r y

Hey there! I'm Mercury, the speedster of the solar system!

Overview

Mercury is the smallest planet in our solar system and the closest to the Sun. It is only slightly larger than Earth's Moon. Mercury's surface experiences extreme temperature fluctuations, from extremely hot to extremely cold.

Physical Characteristics

  • Diameter: 4,880 km
  • Mass: 3.30 × 10^23 kg
  • Average Temperature: 167 °C (day), -173 °C (night)

Orbit & Rotation

  • Orbital Period: 88 Earth days
  • Rotation Period: 59 Earth days
  • Distance from Sun: 57.9 million km

Moons

Mercury has no moons.

Atmosphere

Thin Atmosphere: Mercury has a very thin atmosphere (exosphere) composed mostly of oxygen, sodium, hydrogen, helium, and potassium. Its exosphere is constantly replenished by solar wind and micrometeoroid impacts.

Temperature Extremes: Due to its thin atmosphere, Mercury experiences extreme temperature variations, ranging from about 430°C (800°F) on the sunlit side to -180°C (-290°F) in shadowed regions.

Interesting Facts

Unusual Orbit: Mercury has a highly eccentric orbit, meaning it's not perfectly circular but rather elongated. This results in significant changes in its distance from the Sun during its orbit.


Slow Rotation: Mercury has a slow rotational period compared to its orbit around the Sun. It rotates on its axis once every 59 Earth days, but completes an orbit around the Sun in about 88 Earth days.

Summary

Mercury, despite its small size and proximity to the Sun, exhibits fascinating geological features, extreme environmental conditions, and a history of exploration that has unveiled many of its mysteries. Understanding these aspects helps us appreciate the complexity and uniqueness of our solar system's smallest planet.
