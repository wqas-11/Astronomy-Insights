H a u m e a

Aloha! I'm Haumea, the elongated wonder spinning through the Kuiper Belt.

Overview

Haumea is a dwarf planet located beyond Neptune's orbit in the Kuiper Belt. It is unique due to its elongated shape, which is thought to be the result of its rapid rotation. Haumea is named after the Hawaiian goddess of childbirth.

Physical Characteristics

  • Diameter: 1,960 x 1,518 x 996 km (due to its elongated shape)
  • Mass: 4.01 × 10^21 kg
  • Average Temperature: -241 °C

Orbit & Rotation

  • Orbital Period: 283 Earth years
  • Rotation Period: 3.9 hours
  • Distance from Sun: 6.5 billion km

Moons

Haumea has two known moons, Hi'iaka and Namaka.

Atmosphere

Thin Atmosphere: Haumea is too distant to have a well-studied atmosphere, and its fast rotation and elongated shape complicate direct observation.

Temperature Extremes: Haumea's surface temperature is extremely cold, averaging around -241°C (-402°F).

Exploration History

Observations: Haumea has been studied primarily through telescopic observations and has not been visited by spacecraft.

Interesting Facts

Elongated Shape: Haumea's rapid rotation causes it to have an elongated, egg-like shape.

Rings: Haumea has a ring system, the first discovered around a dwarf planet.

Moons: Haumea has two known moons, Hi'iaka and Namaka.

Summary

Haumea stands out among dwarf planets due to its unique shape and rapid rotation. Its ring system and moons add to its intrigue, making it an interesting target for future studies.
