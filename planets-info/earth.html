E a r t h

Hey there! I'm Earth, your cozy home in the vast universe.

Overview

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only astronomical object known to harbor life. It is home to millions of species, including humans. Earth's atmosphere protects life by absorbing ultraviolet solar radiation and reducing temperature extremes between day and night.

Physical Characteristics

  • Diameter: 12,742 km
  • Mass: 5.97 × 10^24 kg
  • Average Temperature: 15 °C

Orbit & Rotation

  • Orbital Period: 365.25 days (1 Earth year)
  • Rotation Period: Approximately 24 hours (1 Earth day)
  • Distance from Sun: 149.6 million km

Moons

Earth has one natural satellite, the Moon, which plays a significant role in stabilizing its axial tilt and moderating climate.

Atmosphere

Balanced Atmosphere: Earth's atmosphere is composed of 78% nitrogen, 21% oxygen, and trace amounts of argon, carbon dioxide, and other gases. This balance supports life and protects the planet from harmful solar radiation.

Temperature Range: Earth experiences a wide range of temperatures depending on location and season, from around -89.2°C (-128.6°F) in Antarctica to 56.7°C (134°F) in Death Valley, California.

Exploration History

Human Spaceflight: The Apollo missions (1969-1972) by NASA were significant, landing the first humans on the Moon and bringing back samples for study.

Interesting Facts

Water Coverage: About 71% of Earth's surface is covered by water, making it the only known planet with liquid water on its surface.

Life Diversity:Earth is home to millions of species of plants, animals, and microorganisms, supported by a wide variety of ecosystems.

Magnetic Field: Earth's magnetic field protects the planet from the solar wind and cosmic radiation, playing a crucial role in maintaining its atmosphere.

Summary

Earth is a unique and diverse planet with a life-supporting atmosphere and vast ecosystems. Its exploration history has not only advanced human knowledge of space but also deepened our understanding of our home planet's complex systems and environments.
