Hey! I'm Ceres, the largest asteroid and the only dwarf planet in the asteroid belt.

Overview

Ceres is the largest object in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter, and it is the only dwarf planet located in the inner solar system. Ceres is unique because it is both a dwarf planet and an asteroid. It is composed of rock and ice and shows evidence of geologic activity and possibly an ancient ocean beneath its surface.

Physical Characteristics

  • Diameter: 940 km
  • Mass: 9.4 × 10^20 kg
  • Average Temperature: -105 °C

Orbit & Rotation

  • Orbital Period: 4.6 Earth years
  • Rotation Period: 9 hours
  • Distance from Sun: 413.7 million km

Moons

Ceres has no moons.

Atmosphere

Thin Exosphere: Ceres has a very thin exosphere with water vapor detected, likely originating from ice beneath its surface.

Temperature Extremes: Surface temperatures on Ceres range from about -105°C (-157°F) to -34°C (-29°F).

Exploration History

Dawn Mission (2007-2018): NASA's Dawn spacecraft studied Ceres in detail, revealing its surface composition and geological activity.

Interesting Facts

Water Ice: Ceres is believed to contain a large amount of water ice beneath its surface.

Cryovolcanism: Evidence suggests that Ceres may have cryovolcanoes, which erupt water or other volatiles instead of molten rock.

Bright Spots: The bright spots in the Occator Crater are believed to be deposits of sodium carbonate, indicating past hydrothermal activity.

Summary

Ceres, the largest object in the asteroid belt, shows signs of water ice and cryovolcanism, making it a unique object of study. The data from the Dawn mission have greatly expanded our understanding of this dwarf planet's composition and history.
