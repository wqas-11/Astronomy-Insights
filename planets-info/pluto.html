p l u t o

Hi! I'm Pluto, once the ninth planet and now a proud dwarf planet.

Overview

Pluto, once considered the ninth planet of our solar system, is now classified as a dwarf planet. It resides in the Kuiper Belt, a region of the solar system beyond Neptune that is populated by many small, icy bodies. Pluto has a thin atmosphere and a heart-shaped glacier known as Tombaugh Regio.

Physical Characteristics

  • Diameter: 2,377 km
  • Mass: 1.31 × 10^22 kg
  • Average Temperature: -229 °C

Orbit & Rotation

  • Orbital Period: 248 Earth years
  • Rotation Period: 6.4 Earth days
  • Distance from Sun: 5.9 billion km

Moons

Pluto has five known moons: Charon, Styx, Nix, Kerberos, and Hydra. Charon is the largest, nearly half the size of Pluto itself.

Atmosphere

Thin Atmosphere: Pluto has a thin atmosphere composed mainly of nitrogen, with minor amounts of methane and carbon monoxide. This atmosphere can freeze and collapse onto the surface as Pluto moves further from the Sun.

Temperature Extremes: Surface temperatures on Pluto range from about -240°C (-400°F) to -218°C (-360°F), depending on its position in its elliptical orbit.

Interesting Facts

Heart-Shaped Region: Pluto's most famous feature is the heart-shaped region known as Tombaugh Regio, named after Clyde Tombaugh, who discovered Pluto in 1930.

Five Moons: Pluto has five known moons: Charon, Styx, Nix, Kerberos, and Hydra. Charon is the largest, and it is so large relative to Pluto that they are sometimes considered a double dwarf planet system.

Varied Terrain: Pluto has a diverse landscape, including mountains made of water ice, vast plains of nitrogen ice, and possible cryovolcanoes.

Summary

Pluto, once considered the ninth planet, is now classified as a dwarf planet. It has captivated scientists and the public alike with its complex geology and dynamic atmosphere. The New Horizons mission revealed a world far more diverse and dynamic than previously thought, making Pluto a key object of study in understanding the outer solar system.
