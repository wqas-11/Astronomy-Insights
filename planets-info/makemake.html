Hi there! I'm Makemake, the frozen dwarf planet of the outer solar system.

Overview

Makemake is a dwarf planet and one of the largest known objects in the Kuiper Belt, a region of icy bodies beyond Neptune. It was named after the Rapa Nui god of fertility. Makemake has a bright surface, likely covered with methane, ethane, and possibly nitrogen ices.

Physical Characteristics

  • Diameter: 1,434 km
  • Mass: 3.1 × 10^21 kg
  • Average Temperature: -239 °C

Orbit & Rotation

  • Orbital Period: 305 Earth years
  • Rotation Period: 22.5 hours
  • Distance from Sun: 6.85 billion km

Moons

Makemake has one known moon, MK 2, which is much darker than the dwarf planet itself.

Atmosphere

Transient Atmosphere: Makemake may have a temporary atmosphere composed mainly of nitrogen, methane, and possibly ethane, which can form as it moves closer to the Sun.

Cold Surface: Makemake's surface temperature is around -239°C (-398°F).

Exploration History

Observations: Like Haumea, Makemake has been primarily studied through telescopic observations.

Interesting Facts

Methane Ice: Makemake's surface is covered with methane ice, giving it a reddish color.

No Known Moons: Unlike other dwarf planets, Makemake does not have any known moons.

Highly Eccentric Orbit: Its orbit around the Sun is highly eccentric (elliptical), causing significant temperature variations between its closest approach (perihelion) and farthest distance (aphelion).

Summary

Makemake, with its methane ice surface and potential for a transient atmosphere, provides a glimpse into the diversity of objects in the Kuiper Belt. Its characteristics highlight the varied nature of dwarf planets.
