J u p i t e r

Salutations! I'm Jupiter, the mighty king of the planets.

Overview

Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system. It is a gas giant with a mass more than two and a half times that of all the other planets in the solar system combined. Jupiter's atmosphere is mostly hydrogen and helium, and it has the most intense radiation environment in the solar system.

Physical Characteristics

  • Diameter: 139,820 km
  • Mass: 1.90 × 10^27 kg
  • Average Temperature: -145 °C

Orbit & Rotation

  • Orbital Period: 12 Earth years
  • Rotation Period: 10 hours
  • Distance from Sun: 778.5 million km

Moons

Jupiter has 79 known moons, including the four largest: Ganymede, Callisto, Io, and Europa.

Atmosphere

Gas Giant: Jupiter's atmosphere is composed primarily of hydrogen (90%) and helium (10%), with traces of methane, ammonia, water vapor, and other gases.

Temperature Extremes:The upper atmosphere of Jupiter is extremely cold, around -145°C (-234°F), while temperatures increase dramatically closer to the core.

Interesting Facts

Great Red Spot: Jupiter's Great Red Spot is a massive storm larger than Earth, raging for at least 400 years.

Largest Planet: Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system, with a diameter of about 142,984 kilometers (88,846 miles).

Largest Planet: Jupiter has the strongest magnetic field of any planet in the solar system, about 20,000 times stronger than Earth's.

Summary

Jupiter, with its immense size and complex atmospheric dynamics, is a gas giant that offers insights into the formation and evolution of the solar system. Its prominent features, such as the Great Red Spot and numerous moons, make it a fascinating subject for continued exploration.
