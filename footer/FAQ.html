Big Bang Theory

What is the Big Bang Theory?

Answer:The Big Bang Theory is the prevailing cosmological model explaining the origin of the universe, suggesting it began as a singularity approximately 13.8 billion years ago.

What evidence supports the Big Bang Theory?

Answer:Evidence includes the cosmic microwave background radiation, the observed redshift of galaxies, and the abundance of light elements such as hydrogen and helium.

Evolution

How did the universe evolve after the Big Bang?

Answer:After the Big Bang, the universe expanded and cooled, leading to the formation of subatomic particles, simple atoms, stars, galaxies, and eventually the complex structures we see today.

Planets

How many planets are in our solar system?

Answer:There are eight recognized planets in our solar system: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune.

Constellations

What are constellations?

Answer:Constellations are patterns of stars in the night sky that have been identified and named by various cultures throughout history.

How many constellations are there?

Answer:There are 88 officially recognized constellations by the International Astronomical Union.
